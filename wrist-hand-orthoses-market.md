These orthoses are often recommended for conditions requiring rehabilitation or dynamic stabilization. On the other hand, static orthotics are devices that provide rigid support and immobilization without allowing for movement. They are typically used for conditions requiring complete restriction of motion to promote healing. Both types of orthotics play a crucial role in the treatment and management of various wrist and hand injuries.</p></p>
