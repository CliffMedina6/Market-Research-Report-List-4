<p><p>Ankle orthoses can be categorized into two main types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide support and protection while allowing for controlled movement of the ankle joint. They are commonly used for conditions such as ankle sprains or tendon injuries. Static orthotics, on the other hand, are more rigid and are meant to limit movement of the ankle joint. They are often used for conditions like arthritis or instability. Both types of orthoses play a crucial role in rehabilitation and prevention of ankle injuries.</p></p>
