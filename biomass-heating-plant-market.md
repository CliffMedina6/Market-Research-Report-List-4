<p><p>Biomass heating plants utilize various technologies to convert organic materials into heat. Direct combustion involves burning biomass directly to generate heat. Gasification converts biomass into a combustible gas which is then burned for heat. Combined heat and power systems use biomass to simultaneously produce heat and electricity. Other types of biomass heating plants may include anaerobic digestion, pyrolysis, and thermal depolymerization. Each technology offers unique benefits and applications in the renewable energy market.</p></p>
