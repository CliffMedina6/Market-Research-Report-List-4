<p><ul><li>Internal Mix System</li><li>External Mix System</li><li>Fan-Based System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Snow making systems can be categorized into three main types based on their design and operation. Internal mix systems involve mixing water and compressed air inside the nozzle to create small ice particles. External mix systems mix air and water outside the nozzle, producing larger snowflakes. Fan-based systems use high-powered fans to disperse the water particles into the air, resulting in snow formation. Each system has its own advantages and is used in various applications depending on the requirements of the snow-making process.</p></p>
