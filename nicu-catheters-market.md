<p><p>NICU catheters are essential medical devices used in neonatal intensive care units for delivering medication, nutrition, and fluids to critically ill newborns. Different types of NICU catheters include Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICCs), Central Venous Catheters (CVCs), Umbilical Venous Catheters (UVCs), and others. PICCs are inserted into a vein in the arm, while CVCs are placed in a larger vein, typically in the chest or neck. UVCs are inserted into the umbilical vein in newborns. Other types of NICU catheters may include arterial catheters and umbilical arterial catheters.</p></p>
