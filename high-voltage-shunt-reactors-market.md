<p><p>High Voltage Shunt Reactors are used in power transmission systems to regulate voltage levels. They come in two main types: Dry Type and Oil-Immersed Type. Dry Type Shunt Reactors use air as the cooling medium, making them environmentally friendly and suitable for indoor installations. On the other hand, Oil-Immersed Type Shunt Reactors use oil as the cooling medium, providing better heat dissipation and insulation properties, making them ideal for outdoor installations or harsh environments. Both types play a crucial role in maintaining grid stability and ensuring efficient power transmission.</p></p>
