<p><p>Elastoforming presses are classified into two main types based on the driving mechanism: electric and hydraulic. Electric elastoforming presses utilize electric motors to generate force needed for the forming process, offering precise control and energy efficiency. On the other hand, hydraulic elastoforming presses rely on hydraulic systems for force generation, providing high power output for heavy-duty applications. Both types of presses are commonly used in various industries for shaping and forming elastomeric materials to create products like automotive components, seals, and gaskets.</p></p>
