<p><p>Major tranquilizers, also known as antipsychotic medications, are classified into three generations based on their chemical structure and mechanism of action. First generation antipsychotics, also called typical antipsychotics, were developed in the 1950s and 1960s and primarily block dopamine receptors in the brain. Second-generation antipsychotics, or atypical antipsychotics, were introduced in the 1990s and have a broader mechanism of action, affecting multiple neurotransmitters. Third-generation antipsychotics are currently being developed, with a focus on improving efficacy and reducing side effects compared to previous generations.</p></p>
