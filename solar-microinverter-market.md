<p><p>Grid-connected solar microinverters are designed to work in conjunction with the traditional utility grid, allowing excess solar energy to be fed back into the grid for credit or compensation. On the other hand, grid-off solar microinverters are standalone systems that operate independently from the grid, typically used in remote locations or areas where grid connectivity is unreliable. Both types of microinverters offer increased efficiency and flexibility in solar energy systems, providing a more stable and sustainable source of power.</p></p>
