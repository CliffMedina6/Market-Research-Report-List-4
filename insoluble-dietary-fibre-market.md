<p><p>Insoluble dietary fiber market is segmented into various types such as cellulose, hemicellulose, chitin and chitosan, lignin, bran, resistant starch, and others. Cellulose is derived from plant cell walls, hemicellulose is found in cell walls of plants and helps in digestion. Chitin and chitosan are derived from shellfish and insects, serving as dietary fiber. Lignin is found in plant cell walls and provides structural support. Bran comes from cereal grains. Resistant starch resists digestion in the small intestine. Other types include various plant-based fibers.</p></p>
