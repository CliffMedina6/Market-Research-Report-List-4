<p><p>Surgical rasps come in two main types: double-ended and single-ended. Double-ended rasps have cutting surfaces on both ends, allowing for versatile use during surgery. Single-ended rasps, on the other hand, have cutting surfaces on only one end. Double-ended rasps are often preferred for their convenience and efficiency in performing different tasks without needing to switch instruments. Meanwhile, single-ended rasps are simpler in design and are often used for specific procedures where only one cutting surface is needed.</p></p>
