<p><p>Diffractometers are commonly categorized into different types based on their specific applications. X-ray diffractometers utilize X-ray beams to analyze the structure of crystalline materials. Powder diffractometers are used to analyze powdered samples, providing information on their crystal structure. XRD diffractometers focus on determining the atomic and molecular arrangement in a sample. Monocrystalline diffractometers are designed to analyze single crystal samples. Other types of diffractometers include neutron diffractometers, electron diffractometers, and synchrotron diffractometers, each tailored for specific research needs.</p></p>
